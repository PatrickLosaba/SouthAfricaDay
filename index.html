RATIONALE BEHIND THE EVENT

  • South Africa Day is national-building day where South Africans are inspired by the spirit of our                                     common citizenship, values and constitution to forge a unity that is unique to our land and people.
  • Use of the existing foundations to build a prosperous and inclusive future for all South Africans; is what the South Africa Day project seeks to foster and expand on.
  • A day of celebration and community togetherness supported by South African business & driven by ordinary citizens.
  • On the day: projects, celebrations and events aimed at community-building are carried out throughout the nation.
  • We come from a difficult history that alienated entire peoples from a sense of belonging to our country, divided the communities on basis of race and tribal belonging.
  • All South Africans are required to make special effort on a consistent and committed basis, towards lasting and positive nation-building.
  • South Africa Day seeks to be a holistic initiative to overcome those legacies of the past, on an enduring and lasting basis.


THE LEGACY WE WANT TO CELEBRATE

Our country is full of possibility: 

  • A vibrant and creative youth.
  • People who greet one another.
  • People who mourn the passing of others.
  • People who tell incredible jokes.
  • People who help one another.


SIGNIFANCE OF SOUTH AFRICA DAY

  • The day is intended to inspire everyone to participate in building the country and bringing about a renewed sense of optimism and excitement in belonging to South Africa and the future of the country.
  • South Africa Day is a day of purpose, of personal responsibility towards achieving the unification of the people of South Africa and of what can be achieved when everyone works together towards a common goal.
  • The aim is to physically bring together South Africans who would otherwise have never met without special efforts and/or programmes.
  • South Africa Day is intended to bring a sense of urgency to addressing the challenges facing South Africa within the global economy, and those facing the nation in it’s politics, and in the fracturing of social cohesion

PURPOSE OF THE DAY

  

  • South Africa Day is meant to be a journey where all South Africans are invited to contribute towards building and improving their communities through partnerships with organisations that operate within their communities.
  • The day is a high profile campaign that will elevate the role of business in society and improve it’s relationship in people’s hearts and minds.
  • It will also improve relations with government and civil society and hence, will have a very real commercial value in enhancing business confidence and the image of business in South Africa.


VISION

To contribute in building a South African society that will instil pride to the people of South Africa, and that they would have the reason to celebrate and be inspired by as a nation. 

MISSION

To inspire South African to attain greatness that is underpinned by optimism about the country and its future, national unity, national pride, enhancing the image of South Africa, and the building of the South African identity that the people of South African can embrace with great honour, pride and dignity. 

SO, WHAT ARE WE TRYING TO ACHIEVE?

 

  • There is an indomitable spirit that exists in the South African communities and this spirit needs to be harnessed towards the important task of positive nation-building.
  • We need to move from thinking of ourselves as separate islands, disconnected and afloat.
  • South Africa Day fosters a sense of belonging to a bigger whole, something that is much larger than the sum of it’s parts.
  • South Africa Day is intended to galvanise that hope into tangible action towards nation-building and a prosperous future.
  • The day is intended to be as inclusive as possible, encouraging all South African’s to participate.
  • It will promote the idea of good citizenship and encourage all individuals whether foreign or South African to participate in activities, programmes and events that help to build and improve the communities in which they live.
  • This extends to all organisations: schools, businesses, churches and embassies (all will have to operate within their respective communities)


MAIN OBJECTIVE OF SOUTH AFRICA DAY

AIMED AT INSPIRING THE PEOPLE OF SOUTH AFRICA TOWARDS THE FOLLOWING:

  • Celebrating South Africa, it’s people, it’s achievements and it’s possibilities
  • Supporting initiative that will instil pride and a reason to believe in South Africa
  • Promoting national pride and national unity
  • Encouraging the people of South Africa and the country to work towards achieving greatness and exceed the bounds of the possible
  • Becoming positive ambassadors for the country and our communities
  • Creating, developing and supporting various platforms that improve our image as South Africa
  • Building a South African identity that South African’s can be proud of, and that would define who they are as a people or nation that they can aspire to become

AIMED AT INSPIRING THE PEOPLE OF SOUTH AFRICA TOWARDS THE FOLLOWING:

  • Active citizenship in community upliftment in partnership with business and community organisations
  • Promoting and building relationships that embrace inclusiveness and diversity
  • Encouraging participation of community members to contribute to the improvement of their own communities through events, activities and programmes.
  • Empowerment through voluntary participation in community improvement.
  • Encouraging constructive dialogue about critical issues affecting our nation

WHAT?

  1. Media will be asked to come to the event dressed in all black.
  2. Upon arrival, they will go through registration and receive branded arm bands & lanyards with cards with their names. The arm band and lanyards will be in the South African flag colours with the SA day logo.
  3. They will then be ushered by our promoters to the different stations.
  4. Station 1: take a picture and make their pledge (#ilovemysouthafrica) Photo booth with props. Pics go online and instant pictures are printed for the guests.
  5. Station 2: They will then be given an opportunity to go through the art gallery where we will show the beautiful pictures that tell our stories.
  6. Station 3: Opening video to played (SA stories, influencer declarations etc). The presentation from 3 key members of SA day, 3 influencers. The MC to promote the competition for the day.
  7. Station 4: the media will be split into teams of 3. They will then participate in a competition where they create a Display using the props from the props unit to demonstrate the reasons why they love SA.
  8. The winning team, can bring their partners on the trip at the end of the year plus they will get instant hampers. The group with the most likes/retweets etc will WIN on the day.
  9. Media Packs will contain the press pack (info about South Africa day, the influencers & their role), a book, earphones, desk flags etc
